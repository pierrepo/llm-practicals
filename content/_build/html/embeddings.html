Embeddings#

In this section, we’re mentioning words, but in reality we are manipulating tokens.

Explore the multidimensional space#

Viewing embeddings in high-dimensional space is impossible for any human. The web app Embedding Projector projects many dimensions into only 3: it’s easier to see but at the cost of a strong simplification.

  1. Open the Embedding Projector app.

  2. Keep default parameters (Word2Vec 10k). In this case, you’re viewing 10,000 words (tokens) from 200 dimensions projected into 3 dimensions.

  3. In the right panel, search for a word (for instance, “biology”) and set the number of neighbors as “10”:

  1. Find the closest words (tokens).

  2. Try with other words (for instance: “DNA”, “mouse”…).

  3. Click on the “Clear selection” button (top-right corner).

  4. Hover points and select one with a left click.

  5. Find out closest words (tokens) listed on the right panel.

  6. Repeat this operation several times.

Do the (vector) math#

  1. Open the Dash Word Embeddings Arithmetic app.

  2. The idea is to:

    • Start from a word (for instance: “France”)

    • Subtract a word (for instance: “Paris”)

    • Add another a word (for instance: “Tokyo”)

    • Click the “Run” button.

    • Find the nearest word (not from the input word list) in yellow in the right panel. In this case: “Japan”

    • In the above example, we started with a country (“France”), subtracted its capital (“Paris”) and added a new country (“Japan”) to obtain its capital (“Tokyo”).

    • Note: top nearest words are often the ones taken as inputs. We are looking for the nearest word, not provided as inputs.

  1. Try out other word operations for yourself. Here are a few examples to get you started:

  • uncle - man + woman = ?

  • niece - woman + man = ?

  • cars - car + bike = ?

  • train - trains + pen = ?

  • feet - foot + goose = ?

  • mice - mouse + child = ?

  • cucumber - vegetable + fruit = ?

  • sushi - japan + germany = ?

  • beer - bubble + grape = ?

  1. Try to find out other word operations. Share your most interesting findings in a shared document.
